<p><p>Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) are a type of fuel cell that operate at high temperatures, typically above 500°C. These fuel cells can be categorized into three main types based on their design: Planar, Thin film, and Tubular. Planar SOFCs have a flat, compact design, Thin film SOFCs involve thin layers of materials for enhanced performance, and Tubular SOFCs consist of cylindrical tubes. Each of these market types offers unique advantages and applications in various industries such as transportation, power generation, and portable devices.</p></p>
