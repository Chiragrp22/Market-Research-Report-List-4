<p><ul><li>Piezoelectric</li><li>Piezoresistive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wireless accelerometers are commonly categorized into two main types: piezoelectric and piezoresistive. Piezoelectric accelerometers generate electric charge in response to mechanical stress, making them ideal for high-frequency vibration measurements. On the other hand, piezoresistive accelerometers use changes in resistance to measure acceleration, making them better suited for lower frequency applications. Both types of accelerometers are widely used across various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and industrial monitoring for measuring and analyzing vibration and acceleration data.</p></p>
