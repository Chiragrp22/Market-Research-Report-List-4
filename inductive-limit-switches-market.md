<p><p>Inductive limit switches are available in multiple types in the market. Multiple inductive limit switches refer to a system that includes several individual switches that can be connected together to provide multiple sensing points. On the other hand, single inductive limit switches are standalone devices that provide a single sensing point. Both types offer reliable and accurate detection capabilities, with the multiple switches providing more flexibility in monitoring multiple points in a system, while single switches are simpler and more cost-effective for single point applications.</p></p>
