<p><p>Passive Pedestrian Protection Systems are designed to reduce the impact and injuries caused to pedestrians in the event of a collision by absorbing and dispersing energy. Examples include impact-absorbing bumpers and pre-collision systems. Active Pedestrian Protection Systems, on the other hand, include technologies like pedestrian detection and automatic emergency braking that can actively intervene to prevent collisions with pedestrians. Both types of systems aim to improve pedestrian safety on the roads, but through different approaches.</p></p>
